TrackARC Privacy Policy

Effective July 15, 2026

TrackARC is a track & field time and goal tracker. This policy explains what information the app handles and where it goes. The short version: your data stays on your device unless you choose to create an account, and we never sell data or show ads.

Information the app stores

  • Athlete profile: gender, birthday, and optionally years of training. Gender and birthday power the app's age-aware performance predictions.
  • Performance data: the race/practice times you log, their conditions (timing method, surface, spikes, blocks), your goals, and workout check marks.
  • Account details (only if you create an account): your email address and a password.

Where it's stored

Without an account, everything above is stored only in the app's local storage on your own device. It is never transmitted anywhere. Deleting the app (or its site data) deletes it.

With an account (optional), the same data is also saved to your account so it backs up automatically and syncs across your devices. Accounts and synced data are hosted by Google Firebase (Firebase Authentication and Cloud Firestore), which stores data on Google's servers and processes it under Google's Firebase privacy terms. Your password is handled by Firebase Authentication and is never visible to us. Your synced data is readable only by your signed-in account.

Optional public sharing

TrackARC includes an opt-in public profile. If you turn it on (Profile → Sharing), your chosen display name, your logged times and their conditions, and — for prediction features — your gender and birth year become publicly visible to anyone who searches your display name in the app or on the web version, updating live as times are logged. This exists so family and coaches can follow results. It is off by default; turning it off unlists your profile and takes down the times you shared. If you link to a coach with their code, that coach can enter times on your record; coach-entered times are part of the official record and can only be removed by the coach who entered them. Athletes under 13 should only enable sharing with a parent or guardian's permission, and may use a display name that doesn't identify them (e.g. a first name and initial).

What we don't do

  • No advertising and no ad networks.
  • No analytics or tracking of any kind.
  • No selling, renting, or sharing of your information with third parties (Firebase, above, acts only as the storage provider).

Deleting your data

  • On-device data: uninstall the app, or clear the site's data in your browser.
  • Account and cloud data: open the profile screen in the app and use Delete account. This permanently removes your account and everything stored in the cloud, immediately.

Children

TrackARC is made for track & field athletes of all ages, but it is not directed at children under 13. If you are under 13, please use the app without an account, or have a parent or guardian create and manage the account for you.
